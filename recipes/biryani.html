Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian rice dish made with basmati rice, tender chicken, and a blend of fragrant spices such as saffron, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. The chicken is usually marinated in yogurt and spices, then layered with partially cooked rice and slow-cooked (a method called dum). This process allows the flavors to meld beautifully, creating a rich, savory, and slightly spicy dish. It's often garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and served with raita or salad.

Chicken Biryani Ingredients (serves 4-5 people)

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • Chicken – 1 kg (bone-in pieces preferred)
  • Yogurt – 1 cup (thick, plain)
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 2 tablespoons
  • Red chili powder – 2 teaspoons (adjust to spice level)
  • Turmeric powder – ½ teaspoon
  • Coriander powder – 1½ teaspoons
  • Cumin powder – 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala – 1 teaspoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Lemon juice – 2 tablespoons
  • Fresh mint leaves – ½ cup (chopped)
  • Fresh coriander leaves – ½ cup (chopped)
  • Green chilies – 3–4 (slit)

For the Biryani Masala (Gravy):

  • Oil – ½ cup
  • Onions – 3 large (thinly sliced and fried until golden brown)
  • Tomatoes – 2 large (chopped)
  • Potatoes – 2 medium (peeled and cut in half, fried or boiled – optional but traditional in Karachi biryani)
  • Whole spices:
    • Bay leaves – 2
    • Green cardamom – 4
    • Cloves – 5
    • Black peppercorns – 6–8
    • Cinnamon stick – 2-inch piece
    • Star anise – 1
    • Cumin seeds – ½ teaspoon

For the Rice:

  • Basmati rice – 3 cups (aged rice, soaked for 30 minutes)
  • Water – as needed
  • Salt – to taste
  • Whole spices:
    • Bay leaves – 2
    • Cloves – 4
    • Cardamom – 3
    • Cinnamon stick – 1 small piece
    • Shahi jeera (caraway seeds) – ½ teaspoon

For Layering (Dum):

  • Fried onions – ½ cup (for layering)
  • Mint leaves – a handful
  • Coriander leaves – a handful
  • Saffron – a few strands (soaked in 3 tablespoons warm milk)
  • Yellow/orange food color – a pinch (optional, for color)
  • Ghee – 3 tablespoons
  • Lemon slices – a few (optional)

Steps

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a large bowl, mix:

  • 1 kg chicken (bone-in)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1½ tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ cup mint leaves (chopped)
  • ½ cup coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 3–4 slit green chilies
  • Salt to taste

Marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight in the fridge gives deeper flavor).

Step 2: Prepare the Masala (Gravy)

  1. Heat ½ cup oil in a large pot.
  2. Add whole spices (bay leaves, cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon, star anise, cumin seeds) and let them sizzle.
  3. Add 3 large sliced onions and fry until deep golden brown (birista style).
  4. Add 2 chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and oily.
  5. Add the marinated chicken and stir well.
  6. Add ½ cup water, cover, and cook on medium heat until chicken is tender and the masala thickens (about 20–25 minutes).
  7. Optional: Add fried or boiled potatoes at this stage.

The masala should be rich, slightly oily, and thick — not watery.

Step 3: Prepare the Rice

  1. Boil water in a large pot with salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, shahi jeera).
  2. Add soaked basmati rice (3 cups) and cook until 70% done (the grains should be firm).
  3. Drain the water immediately to stop further cooking.

Step 4: Layer the Biryani (Dum Process)

In a large heavy-bottom pot:

  1. Add a layer of cooked chicken masala at the bottom.
  2. Add a layer of rice on top.
  3. Sprinkle a little saffron milk, fried onions, mint, coriander, and a few drops of ghee or food color.
  4. Repeat the layers until everything is used up (end with a rice layer).

Step 5: Steam (Dum) the Biryani

  1. Cover the pot tightly with a lid (or seal with dough to trap steam).
  2. Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then on low heat for 20–25 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let it rest (don’t open yet) for 10 minutes.

Step 6: Serve & Enjoy!

Gently mix the layers before serving.

Serve hot with:

  • Raita (mint or cucumber yogurt sauce)
  • Salad
  • Lemon wedges

The result is flavorful rice and tender chicken with a nice mix of spices.
