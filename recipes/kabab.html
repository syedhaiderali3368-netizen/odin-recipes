Chapli Kabab

Description

Chapli Kabab is a famous Pakistani minced meat dish that comes from Peshawar. It’s made with ground beef or mutton mixed with spices, herbs, and tomatoes, then shaped into flat patties and shallow-fried until crispy on the outside and juicy inside.

The kababs have a bold, spicy flavor with hints of coriander, cumin, and pomegranate seeds that make them really special. They are usually served hot with naan, yogurt sauce, or green chutney, and are perfect for lunch, dinner, or a weekend BBQ.

Chapli Kabab Ingredients (serves 4–5 people):

Main Ingredients:
  • Minced beef or mutton – 500 g (preferably with a little fat for juiciness)
  • Onion – 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Tomato – 1 medium, finely chopped (some people press slices on top before frying)
  • Green chilies – 2–3, finely chopped
  • Fresh coriander leaves – 2 tablespoons, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves – 1 tablespoon, chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tablespoon
  • Gram flour (besan) – 2 tablespoons (lightly roasted; helps bind)
  • Egg – 1 (for binding)
  • Cornmeal (makai ka atta) – 1 tablespoon (optional, adds crispiness)

Spices:

  • Red chili flakes – 1 teaspoon
  • Crushed coriander seeds – 1½ teaspoons
  • Crushed cumin seeds – 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala – 1 teaspoon
  • Pomegranate seeds (anardana) – 1 tablespoon, crushed (signature flavor!)
  • Salt – to taste
  • Black pepper – ½ teaspoon

For Frying:

  • Ghee or oil – as needed, for shallow frying

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Mince Mixture

  1. In a large bowl, add:
    • 500 g minced beef (with a little fat)
    • 1 finely chopped onion
    • 1 finely chopped tomato
    • 2–3 finely chopped green chilies
    • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
    • 1 tbsp chopped mint leaves
    • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tbsp roasted gram flour (besan)
    • 1 egg
    • 1 tbsp crushed pomegranate seeds (anardana)
    • 1½ tsp crushed coriander seeds
    • 1 tsp crushed cumin seeds
    • 1 tsp chili flakes
    • 1 tsp garam masala
    • ½ tsp black pepper
    • Salt to taste
  2. Mix everything gently using your hands until all ingredients are well combined. (Tip: Don’t overmix or the kababs will become tough.)

Step 2: Shape the Kababs

  1. Wet your hands slightly to prevent sticking.
  2. Take a portion of the mixture and shape it into a flat, round patty — about the size of your palm.
  3. You can press a small tomato slice on top for an authentic look (optional).

Step 3: Fry the Kababs

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a flat frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Carefully place the kababs in the pan — don’t overcrowd.
  3. Fry each side for about 4–5 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through.
  4. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Step 4: Serve & Enjoy

Serve the Chapli Kababs hot with:

  • Naan or paratha
  • Mint chutney or yogurt raita
  • Lemon wedges and sliced onions

The kababs turn out crispy on the outside and juicy inside, with a bold, spicy flavor.
